The Risk of Acetyldehyde


Acetyldehyde have many function, but it will make the negative impact if it used by the short-term or the long-term. This is the negative impact from use the acetaldehyde:


1. Inhaled


For the short-term : low-concentration steam can cause cough with burning of the nose and throat. Higher concentrations may lead to delayed pulmonary edema beginning with symptoms of joy followed by narcosis accompanied by symptoms of headache, drowsiness, dizziness. Other effects include severe irritation of the mucous membranes, throat, chest pain, bronchitis, difficulty breathing, nausea, vomiting, malaise
For the long-term : May resemble the symptoms of chronic alcoholism (alcoholism) with weight loss, anemia, delirias (state of mental excitement, confusion and consciousness decline), visual and auditory hallucinations,decreased intelligence and psychological disorders.


2. Contact with eyes


For the short-term : Mild irritation, burning, blurred vision, abnormal sensitivity to light (photophobia) and foreign feelings towards the body. Direct contact with liquid acetaldehyde may cause injury to a painful cornea and possible burns.
For the long-term : May cause inflammation of the lining of the eye. High concentrations and sustained exposure can injure the corneal epithelium, resulting in an alien feeling to the body, an abnormal sensitivity to light (photophobia), as well as permanent lacrimation.


3. Consumed


For the short-term : May cause nausea, vomiting, diarrhea and abdominal pain. Damage to mucous membranes, narcosis accompanied by headache, drowsiness, dizziness, loss of consciousness, coma and failure of respiratory system
For the long-term : May resemble the symptoms of chronic alcoholism (alcoholism) with weight loss, anemia, delirias (state of mental excitement, confusion and loss of consciousness), visual and auditory hallucinations, decreased intelligence and psychological disorders. Mice given acetaldehyde in drinking water show the onset of hyperkeratosis in the front of the stomach.


4. Contact with skin


For the short-term : May cause redness and irritation. Direct contact with the ingredients may cause dermatitis which makes skin sensitized, occurring in individuals who have been exposed to acetaldehyde previously. Intensive contact with liquid acetaldehyde may cause erythema and burns.
For the long-term : May cause erythema, burns, and dermatitis that make skin sensitive when in contact with the ingredients. Symptoms of dermatitis may occur.
