WHAT IS ACETALDEHID

  • Acetaldehyde, or by its systematic name ethanal, is an organic compound of the aldehyde group, by chemical formula CH3CHO or MeCHO. This compound is a flammable liquid with the smell of fruits. Acetaldehyde is present in cooked fruits and coffee, and fresh bread. These compounds are produced by plants in their normal metabolism. Acetaldehyde is also an intermediate in the production of acetic acid, some esters, and other chemicals.

    ACETAL

    Acetal is the functional group with the following R2C (OR ') 2 connectivity, in which both groups R' are organic fragments. Central carbon atoms have four bonds to it, and are therefore saturated and have tetrahedral geometry. Two groups of R'O may be equivalent to each other or not. Two groups of R can be equivalent to each other ("acetal symmetric") or not ("asset mixture"), and one or both can even be hydrogen atoms rather than organic fragments. Acetals are formed from and converted into carbonyl compounds (aldehydes or ketones R2C = O). The term ketal is sometimes used to identify structures associated with ketones and not aldehydes and, historically, the term acetal is used specifically for the case of aldehydes.

    USE

    Used in the manufacture of paraldehyde, acetic acid, butanol, perfume, flavoring agent, aniline dye, plastics, synthetic rubber, silver glass, gelatin fiber hardeners. As an intermediary on pesticide and photographic formulations, cellulose acetate, vinyl acetate resin, acetate ester, synthetic pyridine derivative and terephthalic acid.

    STORAGE

    Should be tightly closed, not flammable. Protect from physical damage. Store in sealed and non-flammable packaging, in a cool place (controlled room temperature), inert, cool and dry atmospheric conditions. Storage and handling are performed in separate, well-ventilated buildings. Avoid contact with heat, flame, spark and other sources of ignition. Separate from ingredients that should not be mixed. Store along with flammable liquids. Can form explosive peroxide. Avoid contact with light. Monitor inhibitor content. Do not evaporate or distill to dry.
