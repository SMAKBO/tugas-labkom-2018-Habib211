History Of Acetaldehyde

History

History :

Acetaldehyde was first made by Scheele in 1774 through dehydrogenation of ethyl alcohol and was known as a new compound in 1800 by Foureroy and Vauquelin. In 1835, Liebig determined the new compound with the name aldehyde derived from the Latin al (alcohol) dehyd (rogenated). Kutscherow in 1881 investigated the formation of acetaldehyde by the addition of water to acetylene.

History of the Acetaldehyde Production Process

>In general, the acetaldehyde production processes include: oxidation or dehydrogenation of ethanol, acetylene hydration and saturated hydrocarbon oxidation or acetylene. Acetaldehyde production peaked in 1969 with a total of 1.65 billion pounds. The production is 42% made from ethylene oxidation directly. But with more cost-saving processes and maximizing the product, the ethanol dehydrogenation process using Cu.Cr (Chrommium-copper active) catalyst is used.
